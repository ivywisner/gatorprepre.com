How Long Does It Take to Get a Florida Real Estate License?

For most people: 6 to 14 weeks, start to finish. The course is the long pole, the state moves faster than you'd expect, and almost every delay on the list is one you can prevent. Here's where the time actually goes.

The Short Answer

Six Weeks If You Push, A Few Months If Life Happens

Florida's path is one of the faster ones in the country — the required education is 63 hours (compare Texas at 180 or California at 135 with mandatory pacing rules), and nothing in the process forces long waits. A motivated person who studies full-time and files paperwork promptly can realistically go from "enrolled" to "licensed" in about six weeks. Most people, balancing jobs and actual lives, land somewhere in the 2–3 month range.

Here's the whole pipeline, step by step. A useful trick before we start: several steps can overlap — you can submit your application and fingerprints while still finishing the course, and that overlap is the single biggest timeline compression available.

1
The 63-Hour Pre-License Course
3–8 weeks typical

FREC-approved, and fully available online at your own pace. This is where the timeline is made or lost: at ~2 hours a day it's about five weeks; nights-and-weekends people usually take six to eight. The course ends with a school-administered final exam — and note that failing that final twice means repeating the course, so don't sprint past the material. Your completion certificate is valid for two years.

2
DBPR Application + Fingerprints
Can overlap step 1

File the application online ($62.75) and get fingerprinted through an approved vendor. Neither requires a finished course — starting these while you're mid-course is the pro move, so the state's clock runs while yours does.

3
DBPR Review → Authorization to Test
2–4 weeks typical

DBPR reviews your application and background check, then emails your authorization. Typical turnaround is 10–30 days for clean applications; Florida law caps the review at 90 days, but most people never get near that. Applications with criminal history disclosures or missing documents take longer — completeness is the speed hack. Your exam eligibility is good for two years once issued.

4
Schedule + Take the Exam
Days to 2 weeks

Book online with Pearson VUE — test centers across Florida generally have availability within days to a couple of weeks. The exam is 100 questions, 3.5 hours, 75 to pass, in person at a test center. You get your result the same day, and if it goes wrong, retakes can happen within 24 hours.

5
Activate the License
Fast

Pass, and your license issues in an inactive state — it activates when a broker employs you (or you file as active with a brokerage). Lining up a brokerage while you study means this step costs zero calendar days.

📸

Snapshot — July 2026

DBPR reviews are running in the normal 2–4 week band, and Pearson VUE seats are not a bottleneck. Reports from recent applicants put the common full journey at 6–14 weeks. Processing speeds drift over time — for today's status, DBPR's MyFloridaLicense portal shows your application status directly.

Where Timelines Die

The Avoidable Delays

Nearly every horror story traces to one of four causes: an incomplete application (missing transcript, unsigned form — instant weeks-long detour), waiting to file until the course is done (serializing steps that stack fine in parallel), failing the school final twice (that's a course repeat), or failing the state exam — which half of first-timers do, and which adds a $57.75 fee and however long your re-prep takes.

💡

The cheapest week you'll ever save is the retake you never need. The exam is the only step where preparation directly buys back calendar time — walking in over-prepared is the single highest-leverage move in the whole pipeline.
